AP World History: Modern

This path teaches world history as patterns across regions: state-building, networks, empire, revolution, industrialization, conflict, decolonization, and globalization.

Official course/reference page

Prerequisites

  • Basic world geography
  • Chronology from c. 1200–present
  • Ability to compare across regions

Course skills

  • Contextualization, sourcing, comparison, causation, continuity/change over time.
  • Trace global networks, states, empires, migrations, technologies, revolutions, and ideologies.
  • Write SAQ, DBQ, and LEQ with global evidence and complexity.
  • Compare regions without overgeneralizing.
  • Use themes: humans/environment, culture, governance, economic systems, social interactions, technology/innovation.

Key Empires 1200–1450
Mongol Empire
Largest contiguous land empire. Pax Mongolica enabled trade. Destroyed Baghdad (1258). Spread Black Death westward. Adopted local cultures (Yuan China, Ilkhanate Islam).
Mali Empire
Mansa Musa's hajj (1324) — displayed enormous wealth, destabilized Mediterranean gold prices. Timbuktu = Islamic learning center. Built on gold-salt trade.
Song China
Champa rice (↑food supply). Gunpowder, printing, compass. Paper money. Eventually conquered by Mongols (Yuan 1271).
Aztec & Inca
Aztec: chinampas, tribute system, Tenochtitlan. Inca: mit'a labor, quipus, road network. Both fell rapidly to Spanish — disease + internal divisions.

1. The Mongol Empire most directly contributed to which development?
A) Spread of Christianity across Asia
B) Pax Mongolica enabling trade AND spread of the Black Death along Silk Road routes
C) Development of oceanic trade routes
D) End of the Islamic Caliphates

Imperialism & Decolonization
Motives (MEN)
M

E

Scramble for Africa
Berlin Conference (1884–85): Europeans divide Africa without African input. By 1914: only Ethiopia (Adwa 1896) and Liberia independent. Colonial economies extract raw materials.
Responses to Imperialism
Sepoy Mutiny (India 1857) → direct crown control. Boxer Rebellion (China 1900). Meiji Restoration (Japan 1868): industrialize to resist. Ethiopian victory at Adwa (1896).
Meiji Japan
Successful modernization in response to imperialism. Adopted Western technology, military organization, education. Became imperial power — won Sino-Japanese War (1894) and Russo-Japanese War (1905).

A) It inspired the French Revolution
B) It was the only successful slave revolution resulting in an independent Black republic

Period 4 (1900–Present)
World Wars, Cold War & Globalization
25% of exam
🌍
20th Century Overview
WWI (1914–1918)
MAIN: Militarism, Alliances, Imperialism, Nationalism. ~20 million dead. Collapse of Ottoman, Austro-Hungarian, Russian, German empires. Sykes-Picot divided Middle East. Treaty of Versailles → seeds of WWII.
WWII (1939–1945)
Rise of fascism. Holocaust — 6 million Jews + millions others. ~70–85 million dead. Atomic bombs on Japan. Decolonization accelerates. Cold War begins. UN founded.
Cold War
US (capitalism) vs. USSR (communism). Truman Doctrine; Marshall Plan; NATO vs. Warsaw Pact. Proxy wars: Korea, Vietnam, Angola, Afghanistan. Non-Aligned Movement.
Decolonization
India (1947) — Gandhi's nonviolence; Partition. Africa (1950s–70s) — 'Wind of Change.' Artificial colonial borders → conflict. Cold War superpowers compete for influence.

SPICE-T Themes
AP World History Themes
Apply to Every Period
🌍
The 5 SPICE-T Themes
S — Social
Class, gender, family, ethnic/racial groups. How societies organize relationships and hierarchies.
P — Political
States, governments, empires, revolutions, nationalism. How power is organized and contested.
I — Interactions
Trade, migration, warfare, diplomacy, environment-human interactions.
C — Culture
Belief systems, religion, philosophy, art, science. How ideas spread and change.
E — Economy
Agriculture, labor, trade, industrialization. Production and exchange of goods.
T — Technology
Innovations in agriculture, military, communication, transportation.
DBQ/LEQ Essay Strategy
Thesis:
Make a historically defensible claim with a line of reasoning — HOW and WHY, not just what.
Contextualization:
Describe broader historical context BEFORE the prompt's time frame — a full developed paragraph.
HAPP Sourcing:
For 2+ documents, explain how Historical situation, Audience, Purpose, or Point of view affects the argument.
Complexity:
Connect to another period, geographic area, or demonstrate nuanced causation.

1. Contextualization in the AP World DBQ requires:
A) A brief mention of earlier events in your introduction
B) A developed description of broader historical context that CONNECTS to the prompt — more than a phrase

Meiji Japan = successful modernization in response to imperialism
Haitian Revolution = first Black republic, only successful slave revolution
Berlin Conference (1884): Europeans divided Africa without African input
SPICE-T: Social, Political, Interactions, Culture, Economy, Technology
Contextualization = developed paragraph, not a phrase
HAPP sourcing for DBQ = Historical situation, Audience, Purpose, Point of view
Complexity point: connect to another period, region, or demonstrate nuanced causation

<script>function pick(el, isCorrect, qId) {
  const qItem = el.closest('.q-item');
  if (qItem.classList.contains('answered')) return;
  qItem.classList.add('answered');
  el.classList.add(isCorrect ? 'correct' : 'wrong');
  if (!isCorrect) {
    qItem.querySelectorAll('.q-choice[data-correct="true"]').forEach(c => c.classList.add('show-correct'));
  }
  qItem.querySelectorAll('.q-choice').forEach(c => c.classList.add('picked'));
  const explain = document.getElementById('exp-' + qId);
  if (explain) explain.classList.add('show');
  // update score
  const quiz = el.closest('.quiz-section');
  const answered = quiz.querySelectorAll('.q-item.answered').length;
  const correct = quiz.querySelectorAll('.q-choice.correct').length;
  const total = quiz.querySelectorAll('.q-item').length;
  const scoreEl = quiz.querySelector('.quiz-score');
  if (scoreEl) scoreEl.textContent = correct + '/' + answered + ' correct (' + total + ' total)';
}
function resetQuiz(btn) {
  const quiz = btn.closest('.quiz-section');
  quiz.querySelectorAll('.q-item').forEach(q => {
    q.classList.remove('answered');
    q.querySelectorAll('.q-choice').forEach(c => c.classList.remove('correct','wrong','show-correct','picked'));
    q.querySelectorAll('.q-explain').forEach(e => e.classList.remove('show'));
  });
  const scoreEl = quiz.querySelector('.quiz-score');
  if (scoreEl) scoreEl.textContent = '0/0 correct';
}
</script>
