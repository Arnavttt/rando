AP Precalculus

A function-centered AP Precalculus path that builds the symbolic, graphical, numerical, and verbal reasoning needed for college mathematics and sciences. Units 1-3 are AP Exam assessed; Unit 4 is included for completeness but is not assessed on the AP Exam.

18lessons

Start here

Status: New AP course added in the same chunked interactive format.

Suggested pacing: Full-year or compressed semester path; prioritize Units 1-3 for AP Exam preparation and use Unit 4 as enrichment/foundation.

Official course/reference page

Prerequisites

  • Strong Algebra 2 skills: factoring, solving equations, function notation, graph interpretation.
  • Comfort with calculator graphing, tables, regression/modeling, and exact values.
  • Willingness to explain functions in words, not just manipulate equations.

Course skills

  • Model real-world data with functions.
  • Move among equations, tables, graphs, and verbal descriptions.
  • Analyze rates of change, transformations, inverses, and restrictions.
  • Use technology strategically and justify conclusions.
