AP Physics 1 & 2

This path teaches physics by modeling systems with forces, energy, momentum, rotation, waves, electricity, fluids, thermodynamics, optics, and modern physics. Each lesson ties concepts to equations and graphs.

Official course/reference page

Prerequisites

  • Algebra and trigonometry
  • Graph interpretation
  • Comfort modeling word problems

Course skills

  • Model physical systems with diagrams, equations, graphs, and words.
  • Use algebra, trig, vectors, proportional reasoning, slopes, areas, and units.
  • Apply conservation laws, Newtonian reasoning, field models, wave models, and circuit/optics/quantum models.
  • Design experiments and analyze uncertainties, controls, slopes/intercepts, and limitations.
  • Justify answers qualitatively and quantitatively.

Newton's Laws
Newton's 1st Law (Inertia)
Objects remain at rest or in constant velocity unless acted on by a net force. Inertia ∝ mass.
Newton's 2nd Law
Fₙₑₜ = ma. Net force = mass × acceleration. If forces are balanced (Fₙₑₜ=0), object is in equilibrium (a=0).
Newton's 3rd Law
Every action has an equal and opposite reaction. Forces are equal in magnitude, opposite in direction, but act on DIFFERENT objects.
Friction
Static friction (fₛ ≤ μₛN) prevents motion. Kinetic friction (fₖ = μₖN) acts on moving objects. μₛ > μₖ always.

Energy & Work
Concept
Formula
Notes
Work
W = Fd cosθ
θ = angle between force and displacement. W=0 if perpendicular.
Kinetic Energy
KE = ½mv²
Scalar. Always ≥ 0.
Gravitational PE
PE = mgh
h measured from reference point
Work-Energy Theorem
Wₙₑₜ = ΔKE
Net work = change in kinetic energy
Conservation of Energy
KE₁ + PE₁ = KE₂ + PE₂
Only when no non-conservative forces (no friction)
Power
P = W/t = Fv
Rate of work done
📝 Unit Quiz
0/0 correct
Reset
1. A 2 kg box slides down a frictionless ramp from height 5 m. What is its speed at the bottom? (g=10)
A) 10 m/s
B) 50 m/s
C) 5 m/s
D) 100 m/s
A: 10 m/s.
Conservation of energy: mgh = ½mv². g cancels: v = √(2gh) = √(2×10×5) = √100 = 10 m/s. The mass cancels — all objects on a frictionless ramp reach the same speed regardless of mass.
2. An object in circular motion at constant speed is:
A) Not accelerating
B) Accelerating toward the center of the circle
C) Accelerating away from the center
D) In equilibrium
B is correct.
Centripetal acceleration: a = v²/r, directed toward the center. Even at constant SPEED, the velocity vector is changing direction — this IS acceleration. The centripetal force (Fc = mv²/r) is not a new force — it's the NET force directed toward center.

Concept
Formula
Notes
Ohm's Law
V = IR

Opposite of resistor rule

Waves & Sound
Wave Equations
v = fλ
— wave speed = frequency × wavelength
f = 1/T
— frequency = 1/period
Transverse waves: vibration perpendicular to propagation (light, water waves).
Longitudinal waves: vibration parallel to propagation (sound, P-waves).
Sound speed: ~340 m/s in air. Faster in solids/liquids than gases.
Doppler Effect: approaching source → higher frequency (blueshift). Receding → lower (redshift).
📝 Unit Quiz
0/0 correct
Reset
1. Two 6Ω resistors are connected in parallel. The equivalent resistance is:
A) 12Ω
B) 6Ω
C) 3Ω
D) 1Ω
C: 3Ω.
Parallel: 1/R = 1/6 + 1/6 = 2/6 = 1/3 → R = 3Ω. For two equal resistors in parallel, the equivalent resistance is always HALF of one resistor.
2. A wave has frequency 500 Hz and wavelength 0.68 m. Its speed is:
A) 735 m/s
B) 0.00136 m/s
C) 340 m/s
D) 68 m/s
C: 340 m/s.
v = fλ = 500 × 0.68 = 340 m/s. This is approximately the speed of sound in air — the numbers were chosen to reflect this.

Fₙₑₜ = ma — net force, not any individual force
Conservation of energy: mgh = ½mv² on frictionless surfaces (mass cancels)
Centripetal acceleration = v²/r, directed toward center
Series: same current; Parallel: same voltage
Parallel resistors: equivalent resistance LESS than smallest
Series capacitors: equivalent capacitance LESS than smallest
v = fλ for all waves
Newton's 3rd: equal and opposite forces act on DIFFERENT objects
Work = Fdcosθ — zero work if force perpendicular to displacement

<h3 class="sub"><span class="sub-icon">⚡</span>Kinematics — The Big 4 Equations</h3><table class="ctable"><tr><th>Equation</th><th>Variables</th><th>Use When</th></tr><tr><td>v = v₀ + at</td><td>v, v₀, a, t</td><td>No displacement needed</td></tr><tr><td>x = v₀t + ½at²</td><td>x, v₀, a, t</td><td>No final velocity needed</td></tr><tr><td>v² = v₀² + 2ax</td><td>v, v₀, a, x</td><td>No time needed</td></tr><tr><td>x = ½(v₀ + v)t</td><td>x, v₀, v, t</td><td>No acceleration needed</td></tr></table><h3 class="sub"><span class="sub-icon">⚡</span>Newton's Laws</h3><div class="card-row"><div class="card ac-top"><h4>Newton's 1st Law (Inertia)</h4><p>Objects remain at rest or in constant velocity unless acted on by a net force. Inertia ∝ mass.</p></div><div class="card ac-top"><h4>Newton's 2nd Law</h4><p>Fₙₑₜ = ma. Net force = mass × acceleration. W=0 if perpendicular.</td></tr><tr><td>Kinetic Energy</td><td>KE = ½mv²</td><td>Scalar. <div class="quiz-body"><div class="q-item">
<div class="q-text">1. A 2 kg box slides down a frictionless ramp from height 5 m. What is its speed at the bottom? (g=10)</div>
<ul class="q-choices"><li class="q-choice" data-correct="true" onclick="pick(this,true,'phys_m_q0')">A) 10 m/s</li>
<li class="q-choice" data-correct="false" onclick="pick(this,false,'phys_m_q0')">B) 50 m/s</li>
<li class="q-choice" data-correct="false" onclick="pick(this,false,'phys_m_q0')">C) 5 m/s</li>
<li class="q-choice" data-correct="false" onclick="pick(this,false,'phys_m_q0')">D) 100 m/s</li>
</ul>
<div class="q-explain show" id="exp-phys_m_q0" style="display:none"><strong>A: 10 m/s.</strong> Conservation of energy: mgh = ½mv². g cancels: v = √(2gh) = √(2×10×5) = √100 = 10 m/s. The mass cancels — all objects on a frictionless ramp reach the same speed regardless of mass.</div>
</div><div class="q-item">
<div class="q-text">2. An object in circular motion at constant speed is:</div>
<ul class="q-choices"><li class="q-choice" data-correct="false" onclick="pick(this,false,'phys_m_q1')">A) Not accelerating</li>
<li class="q-choice" data-correct="true" onclick="pick(this,true,'phys_m_q1')">B) Accelerating toward the center of the circle</li>
<li class="q-choice" data-correct="false" onclick="pick(this,false,'phys_m_q1')">C) Accelerating away from the center</li>
<li class="q-choice" data-correct="false" onclick="pick(this,false,'phys_m_q1')">D) In equilibrium</li>
</ul>
<div class="q-explain show" id="exp-phys_m_q1" style="display:none"><strong>B is correct.</strong> Centripetal acceleration: a = v²/r, directed toward the center. Even at constant SPEED, the velocity vector is changing direction — this IS acceleration. The centripetal force (Fc = mv²/r) is not a new force — it's the NET force directed toward center.</div>
</div></div>

<h3 class="sub"><span class="sub-icon">⚡</span>Electric Circuits</h3><table class="ctable"><tr><th>Concept</th><th>Formula</th><th>Notes</th></tr><tr><td>Ohm's Law</td><td>V = IR</td><td>V=voltage (volts), I=current (amps), R=resistance (ohms)</td></tr><tr><td>Power</td><td>P = IV = I²R = V²/R</td><td>Power dissipated in resistor</td></tr><tr><td>Series Resistors</td><td>R_total = R₁ + R₂ + ...</td><td>Same current through all; voltages add</td></tr><tr><td>Parallel Resistors</td><td>1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂</td><td>Same voltage across all; currents add</td></tr><tr><td>Capacitance</td><td>C = Q/V</td><td>Charge stored per volt</td></tr><tr><td>Series Capacitors</td><td>1/C_total = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂</td><td>Opposite of resistor rule</td></tr><tr><td>Parallel Capacitors</td><td>C_total = C₁ + C₂</td><td>Opposite of resistor rule</td></tr></table><h3 class="sub"><span class="sub-icon">⚡</span>Waves & Sound</h3><div class="callout"><div class="callout-lbl">Wave Equations</div><ul><li><strong>v = fλ</strong> — wave speed = frequency × wavelength</li><li><strong>f = 1/T</strong> — frequency = 1/period</li><li>Transverse waves: vibration perpendicular to propagation (light, water waves).</li><li>Longitudinal waves: vibration parallel to propagation (sound, P-waves).</li><li>Sound speed: ~340 m/s in air. <script>function pick(el, isCorrect, qId) {
  const qItem = el.closest('.q-item');
  if (qItem.classList.contains('answered')) return;
  qItem.classList.add('answered');
  el.classList.add(isCorrect ? 'correct' : 'wrong');
  if (!isCorrect) {
    qItem.querySelectorAll('.q-choice[data-correct="true"]').forEach(c => c.classList.add('show-correct'));
  }
  qItem.querySelectorAll('.q-choice').forEach(c => c.classList.add('picked'));
  const explain = document.getElementById('exp-' + qId);
  if (explain) explain.classList.add('show');
  // update score
  const quiz = el.closest('.quiz-section');
  const answered = quiz.querySelectorAll('.q-item.answered').length;
  const correct = quiz.querySelectorAll('.q-choice.correct').length;
  const total = quiz.querySelectorAll('.q-item').length;
  const scoreEl = quiz.querySelector('.quiz-score');
  if (scoreEl) scoreEl.textContent = correct + '/' + answered + ' correct (' + total + ' total)';
}
function resetQuiz(btn) {
  const quiz = btn.closest('.quiz-section');
  quiz.querySelectorAll('.q-item').forEach(q => {
    q.classList.remove('answered');
    q.querySelectorAll('.q-choice').forEach(c => c.classList.remove('correct','wrong','show-correct','picked'));
    q.querySelectorAll('.q-explain').forEach(e => e.classList.remove('show'));
  });
  const scoreEl = quiz.querySelector('.quiz-score');
  if (scoreEl) scoreEl.textContent = '0/0 correct';
}
