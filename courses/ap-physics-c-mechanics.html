AP Physics C: Mechanics

A calculus-based mechanics path for engineering/physical-science students. The site path emphasizes representations, derivations, lab thinking, units, signs, calculus, and AP-style handwritten FRQ reasoning.

28lessons

Start here

Status: New AP course added in the same chunked interactive format.

Suggested pacing: Full year if paired with E&M, or semester mechanics path; require frequent problem sets plus derivations and labs.

Official course/reference page

Prerequisites

  • Concurrent or prior calculus is strongly recommended.
  • Strong algebra, trigonometry, vectors, and graph interpretation.
  • Comfort with free-body diagrams, units, and symbolic manipulation.

Course skills

  • Create diagrams, charts, schematics, and graphs.
  • Derive and calculate using calculus-based physics relationships.
  • Compare scenarios and predict functional dependence.
  • Design experiments and justify claims with evidence.
