AP Physics C: Electricity and Magnetism

A calculus-based electricity and magnetism path that builds from electric fields through circuits, magnetism, and induction. Emphasis: vector fields, potential, flux, line/surface integrals, circuit transients, and AP-style derivations.

24lessons

Start here

Status: New AP course added in the same chunked interactive format.

Suggested pacing: Semester or second-half sequence after mechanics; teach with frequent field sketches, integral derivations, and circuit/induction labs.

Official course/reference page

Prerequisites

  • Concurrent or prior calculus.
  • Strong AP Physics C Mechanics fundamentals help but are not always required.
  • Comfort with vectors, dot/cross products, integrals, and differential equations.

Course skills

  • Create field and circuit representations.
  • Use Gauss’s law, potential, Kirchhoff’s rules, Ampere’s law, and Faraday’s law.
  • Derive symbolic relationships and interpret limiting cases.
  • Connect experiments, graphs, and field models.
