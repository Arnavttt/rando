AP European History

A modern European history path from c. 1450 to the present. It teaches content through causation, comparison, continuity/change, contextualization, sourcing, DBQ/LEQ thesis writing, and evidence-based historical argument.

39lessons

Start here

Status: New AP course added in the same chunked interactive format.

Suggested pacing: Full-year history path; combine content lessons with recurring SAQ, DBQ, and LEQ practice.

Official course/reference page

Prerequisites

  • No formal prerequisite.
  • Students should practice reading primary/secondary sources and writing thesis-driven paragraphs.
  • A timeline notebook is strongly recommended.

Course skills

  • Evaluate primary and secondary sources.
  • Analyze claims, evidence, and reasoning.
  • Contextualize historical developments and connect them over time.
  • Develop thesis/claim and support it with specific evidence.
