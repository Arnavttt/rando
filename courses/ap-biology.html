Sources of Genetic Variation in Meiosis
1. Crossing Over (Recombination)
During Prophase I, homologs exchange DNA segments at chiasmata. Creates new allele combinations on a single chromosome. Most powerful source of variation — can create virtually unlimited combinations.
2. Independent Assortment
During Metaphase I, which homolog of each pair goes to which pole is random. 2²³ ≈ 8 million possible chromosome combinations in humans (per gamete). Mendel's Law of Independent Assortment reflects this.
3. Random Fertilization
Any sperm (8 million+ combinations) can fertilize any egg (8 million+ combinations) → 64 trillion+ possible genotypes per couple.
4. Nondisjunction
Failure of chromosomes to separate properly. In Meiosis I: homologs don't separate. In Meiosis II: sister chromatids don't separate. Results: aneuploidy. Trisomy 21 (Down syndrome) = extra chromosome 21. Monosomy X (Turner syndrome) = 45, X.

1. Crossing over in meiosis occurs between:
A) Sister chromatids of the same chromosome
B) Homologous chromosomes during Prophase I
C) Any two chromosomes during Anaphase
D) Sister chromatids during Meiosis II
B: Homologous chromosomes during Prophase I.
Crossing over (recombination) occurs when non-sister chromatids of homologous chromosomes exchange corresponding DNA segments at chiasmata during Prophase I. This is genetically significant because it creates new combinations of alleles that didn't exist on either parent chromosome.
2. A cell undergoes nondisjunction during Meiosis I. How many of the resulting gametes will have an abnormal chromosome number?
A) 1 out of 4
B) 2 out of 4
C) All 4
D) 0 out of 4
C: All 4.
Nondisjunction in Meiosis I means homologs fail to separate. Both homologs end up in the same cell. After Meiosis II, ALL 4 resulting gametes are abnormal — 2 will have an extra chromosome (n+1) and 2 will be missing one (n-1). Compare: nondisjunction in Meiosis II only affects 2 of the 4 gametes.
3. Which event in meiosis directly explains Mendel's Law of Independent Assortment?
A) Crossing over during Prophase I
B) Separation of sister chromatids in Anaphase II
C) Random alignment of homologous pairs at Metaphase I
D) Synapsis forming bivalents
C is correct.
Mendel's Law of Independent Assortment states that alleles of different genes are distributed independently. This occurs because during Metaphase I, each homologous pair lines up at the metaphase plate independently — the orientation of one pair doesn't affect any other pair. This produces 2²³ possible gamete types in humans from chromosome assortment alone.
4. During oogenesis (egg formation), meiosis produces:
A) 4 equally-sized egg cells
B) 1 large secondary oocyte and 3 polar bodies
C) 2 haploid cells, each dividing once more
D) 1 egg and 1 polar body
B: 1 egg + 3 polar bodies.
Oogenesis uses unequal cytokinesis to concentrate cytoplasm and nutrients into one cell. Primary oocyte → (Meiosis I) secondary oocyte + 1st polar body. Secondary oocyte → (Meiosis II) egg + 2nd polar body. The 1st polar body may also divide, giving 3 total polar bodies. All polar bodies degenerate.

Genetics & Gene Expression
25–27% of exam
🧬
Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium
Hardy-Weinberg: p² + 2pq + q² = 1 and p + q = 1
p = dominant allele freq; q = recessive allele freq
p² = homozygous dominant; 2pq = heterozygous (carriers); q² = homozygous recessive (shows trait)
5 HW conditions:
Large population, random mating, no mutation, no gene flow (migration), no natural selection.
Exam shortcut:
Always find q from q² first → then p = 1−q → then 2pq for carrier freq.
Incomplete Dominance
Heterozygote is intermediate: red × white = pink. Alleles unchanged — only phenotype appears blended.
Codominance
Both alleles expressed simultaneously: ABO blood type AB shows both A and B antigens.
Sex-Linked
X-linked recessive expressed more in males (only one X, no second copy to mask). Hemophilia, color blindness.
Epistasis
One gene masks the expression of another. ABO blood type masked by Bombay phenotype (h gene).
🧬
Gene Expression
Central Dogma + Regulation
Transcription:
DNA → mRNA (nucleus). RNA polymerase reads 3'→5', synthesizes 5'→3'.
Translation:
mRNA → protein (ribosome). tRNA anticodons match codons. AUG = start. UAA/UAG/UGA = stop.
lac operon:
ON when lactose present (allolactose removes repressor). Negative control.
trp operon:
ON when tryptophan absent (tryptophan activates repressor = off). Negative control.
Eukaryotic regulation:
Enhancers, silencers, transcription factors. Methylation = off. Histone acetylation = on.

Evolution & Ecology
23–27% of exam
🧬
Mechanisms of Evolution
Natural Selection
Heritable variation + differential reproductive success. Directional (one extreme), stabilizing (intermediate), disruptive (both extremes).
Genetic Drift
Random allele frequency changes. Bottleneck effect (population crash) and founder effect (small colonizing group) are forms. Greater in small populations.
Gene Flow
Movement of alleles between populations via migration. Tends to reduce genetic differences between populations.
Speciation
Allopatric: geographic isolation. Sympatric: polyploidy in plants. Prezygotic vs. postzygotic reproductive barriers.
🧬
Ecology — Population Growth
Exponential:
dN/dt = rN
Logistic:
dN/dt = rN × (K−N)/K
// max rate when N = K/2
10% Energy Rule:
Producer → Primary consumer → Secondary → Tertiary
  100%          10%                 1%         0.1%
Trophic level efficiency =
(energy at level n+1 / energy at level n) × 100%

  const total = quiz.querySelectorAll('.q-item').length;
  const scoreEl = quiz.querySelector('.quiz-score');
  if (scoreEl) scoreEl.textContent = correct + '/' + answered + ' correct (' + total + ' total)';
}
function resetQuiz(btn) {
  const quiz = btn.closest('.quiz-section');
  quiz.querySelectorAll('.q-item').forEach(q => {
    q.classList.remove('answered');
    q.querySelectorAll('.q-choice').forEach(c => c.classList.remove('correct','wrong','show-correct','picked'));
    q.querySelectorAll('.q-explain').forEach(e => e.classList.remove('show'));
  });
  const scoreEl = quiz.querySelector('.quiz-score');
  if (scoreEl) scoreEl.textContent = '0/0 correct';
}
