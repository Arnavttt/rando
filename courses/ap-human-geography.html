AP Human Geography

A complete AP Human Geography path focused on spatial thinking, maps/data, population, culture, politics, agriculture, cities, and development. Students learn from fundamentals through FRQ-style explanation using real-world geographic examples.

34lessons

Start here

Status: New AP course added in the same chunked interactive format.

Suggested pacing: Full-year path; teach one lesson at a time; review with maps/data and short FRQ writing after every unit.

Official course/reference page

Prerequisites

  • Comfort reading maps, charts, and short nonfiction sources.
  • Basic world regional awareness is helpful but not required.
  • Practice explaining cause/effect across different geographic scales.

Course skills

  • Analyze geographic concepts, models, theories, and processes.
  • Interpret maps, charts, satellite images, photographs, landscapes, and quantitative data.
  • Explain spatial relationships across local, regional, national, and global scales.
  • Use real-world examples to support AP Human Geography FRQ answers.
