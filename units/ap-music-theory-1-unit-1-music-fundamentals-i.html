Music Fundamentals I — Lesson 1: staff, clefs, pitch names, accidentals; major/minor scales and key signatures

Unit 1 · Lesson 1
Learn on this site

Music theory must be read, heard, written, and analyzed. For every concept, drill the symbol, sound, written notation, and harmonic function.

staff, clefs, pitch names, accidentals

staff, clefs, pitch names, accidentals must be understood visually, aurally, and functionally. Be able to notate it, hear it, sing/play it, identify it in an excerpt, and explain how it functions in a musical context.

Test yourself: teach this idea from memory, give one example, identify one trap, and answer one exam-style question about it.

major/minor scales and key signatures

major/minor scales and key signatures must be understood visually, aurally, and functionally. Be able to notate it, hear it, sing/play it, identify it in an excerpt, and explain how it functions in a musical context.

Test yourself: teach this idea from memory, give one example, identify one trap, and answer one exam-style question about it.

Objectives
  • Explain and apply staff, clefs, pitch names, accidentals.
  • Explain and apply major/minor scales and key signatures.
How this can be tested
  • aural identification
  • sight singing
  • dictation
  • part writing
  • Roman numeral / chord analysis
  • melodic and harmonic error detection
Practice before moving on
  • Write it on staff paper.
  • Sing or play it.
  • Identify it by ear.
  • Analyze it in a short musical excerpt.
