Rhetorical Situation and Claims — Lesson 1: speaker, audience, purpose, context, exigence; thesis and defensible claims

Unit 1 · Lesson 1
Learn on this site

The core question is never “what happened?” but “how do choices create meaning, argument, tone, structure, or effect for a reader/audience?”

speaker, audience, purpose, context, exigence

speaker, audience, purpose, context, exigence is a reading-and-writing skill. Learn what to notice in a text, how to name the author’s or writer’s choice, and how to explain the effect of that choice on meaning, argument, audience, tone, or structure.

Test yourself: teach this idea from memory, give one example, identify one trap, and answer one exam-style question about it.

thesis and defensible claims

thesis and defensible claims is a reading-and-writing skill. Learn what to notice in a text, how to name the author’s or writer’s choice, and how to explain the effect of that choice on meaning, argument, audience, tone, or structure.

Test yourself: teach this idea from memory, give one example, identify one trap, and answer one exam-style question about it.

claims of fact, value, policy, cause

claims of fact, value, policy, cause is a reading-and-writing skill. Learn what to notice in a text, how to name the author’s or writer’s choice, and how to explain the effect of that choice on meaning, argument, audience, tone, or structure.

Test yourself: teach this idea from memory, give one example, identify one trap, and answer one exam-style question about it.

Objectives
  • Explain and apply speaker, audience, purpose, context, exigence.
  • Explain and apply thesis and defensible claims.
  • Explain and apply claims of fact, value, policy, cause.
How this can be tested
  • analyze choices and effects
  • write a defensible thesis
  • select precise evidence
  • explain commentary
  • build a line of reasoning under time pressure
Practice before moving on
  • Annotate for pattern, contrast, and shift.
  • Write a one-sentence thesis.
  • Choose two pieces of evidence and explain how each supports the claim.
  • Revise one sentence for clarity and sophistication.
