Time Signatures
  • Top number = beats per measure. Bottom = which note gets one beat.
  • 4/4 (common time): 4 quarter-note beats per measure.
  • 3/4 (waltz): 3 quarter-note beats. Strong beat on beat 1.
  • 6/8 (compound duple): 2 groups of 3 eighth notes. Felt in 2.
  • Simple vs. compound: Simple (2/4, 3/4, 4/4) — beat divides into 2. Compound (6/8, 9/8) — beat divides into 3.

🎵The Major Scale & Key Signatures

Major Scale Formula: W-W-H-W-W-W-H
  • W = whole step (2 semitones). H = half step (1 semitone).
  • C major: C-D-E-F-G-A-B-C (no sharps or flats — the reference scale).
  • Circle of Fifths: Moving clockwise adds one sharp. Moving counter-clockwise adds one flat.
  • Sharp keys: G(1#), D(2#), A(3#), E(4#), B(5#), F#(6#), C#(7#)
  • Flat keys: F(1♭), B♭(2♭), E♭(3♭), A♭(4♭), D♭(5♭), G♭(6♭), C♭(7♭)
📝 Unit Quiz 0/0 correct
1. The key signature with 3 sharps is:
  • A) A major
  • B) D major
  • C) E major
  • D) B major
A: A major. Sharps order: F#, C#, G#. Three sharps = F#, C#, G# → A major (or F# minor relative). Mnemonic: Father Charles Goes Down And Ends Battle.
2. In 6/8 time, a dotted quarter note lasts:
  • A) 2 beats
  • B) 1.5 beats
  • C) One full group of three eighths = one compound beat
  • D) 3 beats
C is correct. In 6/8, the dotted quarter = 3 eighth notes = one compound beat. 6/8 has 2 compound beats per measure, each divided into 3. This is why 6/8 feels like a lilt or waltz-like groove, not 6 separate eighth-note pulses.

🎵Chord Construction & Roman Numerals

Triads & Seventh Chords
  • Major triad: Root + major 3rd + perfect 5th (M3 + m3 stacked)
  • Minor triad: Root + minor 3rd + perfect 5th (m3 + M3)
  • Diminished triad: Root + minor 3rd + diminished 5th (m3 + m3)
  • Augmented triad: Root + major 3rd + augmented 5th (M3 + M3)
  • Dominant 7th (V7): Major triad + minor 7th (M3 + m3 + m3)
  • Leading tone dim7 (vii°7): Fully diminished — all minor thirds

📝 Unit Quiz 0/0 correct
1. The interval from D up to A is a:
  • A) Perfect 4th
  • B) Major 6th
  • C) Perfect 5th
  • D) Minor 6th
C: Perfect 5th. Count the letter names inclusively: D(1)-E(2)-F(3)-G(4)-A(5) = 5th. Then check quality: D to A = 7 semitones = perfect 5th. Perfect intervals (unisons, 4ths, 5ths, octaves) come from the major scale built on the lower note.
2. In C major, the chord built on scale degree 7 is:
  • A) C major
  • B) G dominant 7th
  • C) B diminished triad
  • D) A minor
C: B diminished. Scale degree 7 in C major = B. Build a triad: B-D-F. B→D = minor 3rd; D→F = minor 3rd. Two stacked minor thirds = diminished triad (vii°). The diminished 5th (tritone) gives it strong tension → resolves up to I.
Unit 3
SATB Voice Leading
Harmony Writing

🎵The 4-Voice Texture: SATB

Voice Ranges
  • Soprano (S): C4–G5 (middle C to high G)
  • Alto (A): G3–C5
  • Tenor (T): C3–G4
  • Bass (B): E2–C4
  • Voices should generally stay in range. Crossing voices (alto above soprano) is a voice-crossing error.

🎵The Absolute Voice Leading Rules — Must Not Break

Voice Leading Rules
  • No parallel perfect 5ths: Two voices moving in the same direction and maintaining a P5 between them. This is the most common error.
  • No parallel perfect octaves: Same principle — two voices moving in parallel octaves.
  • No voice crossing: Alto should never go above soprano; tenor should never go above alto.
  • Resolve the leading tone (7̂): The 7th scale degree MUST resolve up a half step to the tonic. Never double the leading tone.
  • Resolve the chordal 7th DOWN by step: In a V7, the 7th of the chord (4th scale degree) must resolve DOWN by step to the 3rd scale degree (iii or I⁶).
  • No augmented melodic intervals: No voice should leap an augmented 2nd, augmented 4th, etc.
  • Avoid large leaps: Prefer stepwise motion. Leaps of a 6th or 7th are awkward. After a large leap, move by step in the opposite direction.

🎵Cadence Types

Imperfect Authentic Cadence (IAC)V → I, but inverted or soprano not on tonicCadence, but weaker — less final.
Half Cadence (HC)Any chord → VEnding on dominant — feels like a question, pause in middle.

📝 Unit Quiz 0/0 correct
1. Parallel perfect fifths are prohibited in SATB voice leading because:
  • A) They sound dissonant
  • B) They cause two independent voices to lose independence — merging into one sonority instead of two distinct lines
  • C) They violate church rules from the Middle Ages only
  • D) They are too hard to sing
B is correct. The prohibition on parallel 5ths is about voice independence — two voices moving in parallel perfect consonances stop functioning as two lines and merge. 18th-century common practice harmony requires four clearly independent voices, each with its own melodic logic.
2. A V7 chord resolves to I. The chordal 7th (4̂) must move:
  • A) Up a half step to 5̂
  • B) Down a step to 3̂
  • C) Stay as a common tone
  • D) Jump down a 4th
B: Down a step to 3̂. The chordal 7th creates a dissonance (tritone with the 3rd of the V chord) that demands resolution. The 7th resolves down by step — from 4̂ to 3̂. Simultaneously, the leading tone (7̂) resolves UP to 1̂. These opposing resolutions drive the V7→I progression.

📝 Unit Quiz 0/0 correct
1. Sonata form's 'development' section is characterized by:
  • A) Presentation of two contrasting themes in stable keys
  • B) Return of both themes in the tonic key
  • C) Fragmentation and modulation of themes through multiple keys — harmonic instability
  • D) A new, contrasting melody
C is correct. The development section takes thematic material from the exposition and develops it: fragments, sequences, inverts, and passes it through various keys. It creates maximum harmonic tension before the recapitulation's return to stability in the tonic.
2. A deceptive cadence resolves V to:
  • A) I
  • B) IV
  • C) vii°
  • D) vi
D: vi. The deceptive cadence substitutes vi (tonic minor parallel) for the expected I. The bass moves to vi, but the soprano may still resolve normally. It creates a sense of continuation rather than full closure — composers use it to extend phrases.
Perfect Score Checklist
  • Major scale formula: W-W-H-W-W-W-H
  • Circle of fifths: clockwise adds sharps. Counter-clockwise adds flats.
  • Sharp key order: F C G D A E B (Fat Cats Go Down Alleys Eating Bugs)
  • Flat key order: B E A D G C F (Battle Ends And Down Goes Charles's Father)
  • Diatonic triads in major: I ii iii IV V vi vii° (M m m M M m dim)
  • SATB absolute rules: no parallel 5ths/8ths, no voice crossing, resolve leading tone UP
  • Chordal 7th (V7 → I): 4̂ resolves DOWN to 3̂. Leading tone 7̂ resolves UP to 1̂.
  • PAC = V→I root position, soprano on 1̂. HC = ends on V.
  • Deceptive cadence: V→vi (expected I, got vi)
  • Sonata form: Exposition (2 themes) → Development (fragmented/modulating) → Recapitulation (both themes, tonic)
  • Interval mnemonics: P4='Here Comes the Bride', P5='Star Wars', Tritone='Simpsons'
