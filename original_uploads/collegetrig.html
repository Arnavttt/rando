📊Angle Measurement

Degrees vs. Radians
  • Conversion: Degrees × (π/180) = Radians. Radians × (180/π) = Degrees.
  • Half revolution: 180° = π rad. Quarter: 90° = π/2 rad. Full: 360° = 2π rad.
  • Arc length: s = rθ (θ in radians). Area of sector: A = ½r²θ

Prove: sin²θ/(1−cosθ) = 1 + cosθ
1
Work only the LEFT side.
2
sin²θ = 1 − cos²θ (Pythagorean identity).
3
Substitute: (1−cos²θ)/(1−cosθ)
4
Factor numerator: (1−cosθ)(1+cosθ)/(1−cosθ)
5
Cancel: = 1 + cosθ ✓

Unit 5
Law of Sines & Cosines
Applications

📊Choosing the Right Law

Law of Sines: a/sinA = b/sinB = c/sinC
  • Use for: AAS, ASA (two angles + side), SSA (ambiguous case)
  • Ambiguous case (SSA): May have 0, 1, or 2 solutions — always check!
Law of Cosines: c² = a² + b² − 2ab cosC
  • Use for: SAS (two sides + included angle), SSS (all three sides)
  • Solving for angle: cosC = (a² + b² − c²)/(2ab)
  • Area: K = ½ab sinC
Solve triangle: a=8, b=10, C=60° (SAS — use Law of Cosines)
1
c² = a² + b² − 2ab cosC = 64 + 100 − 2(8)(10)cos60°
2
cos60° = 0.5 → 2(8)(10)(0.5) = 80
3
c² = 164 − 80 = 84 → c = √84 ≈ 9.17
4
Use Law of Sines to find remaining angles: sinA/8 = sin60°/9.17
📝 Unit Quiz 0/0 correct
1. Which law applies to SAS (two sides + included angle)?
  • A) Law of Sines
  • B) Law of Cosines
  • C) Pythagorean Theorem
  • D) Either
B: Law of Cosines. SAS requires Law of Cosines. Law of Sines needs an angle-opposite-side pair.
2. In the SSA ambiguous case, if a < b sinA, there are:
  • A) Two solutions
  • B) One solution
  • C) No solution
  • D) Infinitely many solutions
C: No solution. SSA: if a < b sinA, the side is too short to reach the base line — no triangle exists.
Perfect Score Checklist
  • Unit circle: 30-45-60 in all 4 quadrants — non-negotiable memorization
  • ASTC: Q1 All, Q2 Sin, Q3 Tan, Q4 Cos
  • Period = 2π/|B| for sin/cos; π/|B| for tan/cot
  • Phase shift = C/B (RIGHT when positive in f(x−C/B) form)
  • Pythagorean: sin²+cos²=1; 1+tan²=sec²; 1+cot²=csc²
  • Double angle: sin(2θ)=2sinθcosθ; cos(2θ)=cos²θ−sin²θ
  • When proving identities: work ONE side only
  • arcsin: [−π/2,π/2]; arccos: [0,π]; arctan: (−π/2,π/2)
  • Law of Sines for AAS/ASA/SSA. Law of Cosines for SAS/SSS.
  • SSA = ambiguous case: check for 0, 1, or 2 triangles
