⚡Newton's Laws

Newton's 1st Law (Inertia)

Objects remain at rest or in constant velocity unless acted on by a net force. Inertia ∝ mass.

Newton's 2nd Law

Fₙₑₜ = ma. Net force = mass × acceleration. If forces are balanced (Fₙₑₜ=0), object is in equilibrium (a=0).

Newton's 3rd Law

Every action has an equal and opposite reaction. Forces are equal in magnitude, opposite in direction, but act on DIFFERENT objects.

Friction

Static friction (fₛ ≤ μₛN) prevents motion. Kinetic friction (fₖ = μₖN) acts on moving objects. μₛ > μₖ always.

📝 Unit Quiz 0/0 correct
1. A 2 kg box slides down a frictionless ramp from height 5 m. What is its speed at the bottom? (g=10)
  • A) 10 m/s
  • B) 50 m/s
  • C) 5 m/s
  • D) 100 m/s
A: 10 m/s. Conservation of energy: mgh = ½mv². g cancels: v = √(2gh) = √(2×10×5) = √100 = 10 m/s. The mass cancels — all objects on a frictionless ramp reach the same speed regardless of mass.
2. An object in circular motion at constant speed is:
  • A) Not accelerating
  • B) Accelerating toward the center of the circle
  • C) Accelerating away from the center
  • D) In equilibrium
B is correct. Centripetal acceleration: a = v²/r, directed toward the center. Even at constant SPEED, the velocity vector is changing direction — this IS acceleration. The centripetal force (Fc = mv²/r) is not a new force — it's the NET force directed toward center.

⚡Waves & Sound

Wave Equations
  • v = fλ — wave speed = frequency × wavelength
  • f = 1/T — frequency = 1/period
  • Transverse waves: vibration perpendicular to propagation (light, water waves).
  • Longitudinal waves: vibration parallel to propagation (sound, P-waves).
  • Sound speed: ~340 m/s in air. Faster in solids/liquids than gases.
  • Doppler Effect: approaching source → higher frequency (blueshift). Receding → lower (redshift).
📝 Unit Quiz 0/0 correct
1. Two 6Ω resistors are connected in parallel. The equivalent resistance is:
  • A) 12Ω
  • B) 6Ω
  • C) 3Ω
  • D) 1Ω
C: 3Ω. Parallel: 1/R = 1/6 + 1/6 = 2/6 = 1/3 → R = 3Ω. For two equal resistors in parallel, the equivalent resistance is always HALF of one resistor.
2. A wave has frequency 500 Hz and wavelength 0.68 m. Its speed is:
  • A) 735 m/s
  • B) 0.00136 m/s
  • C) 340 m/s
  • D) 68 m/s
C: 340 m/s. v = fλ = 500 × 0.68 = 340 m/s. This is approximately the speed of sound in air — the numbers were chosen to reflect this.
Perfect Score Checklist
  • Big 4 kinematics equations — identify which variable is missing to choose the right one
  • Fₙₑₜ = ma — net force, not any individual force
  • Conservation of energy: mgh = ½mv² on frictionless surfaces (mass cancels)
  • Centripetal acceleration = v²/r, directed toward center
  • Series: same current; Parallel: same voltage
  • Parallel resistors: equivalent resistance LESS than smallest
  • Series capacitors: equivalent capacitance LESS than smallest
  • v = fλ for all waves
  • Newton's 3rd: equal and opposite forces act on DIFFERENT objects
  • Work = Fdcosθ — zero work if force perpendicular to displacement
